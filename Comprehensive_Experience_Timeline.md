  - Supplier installed additional testing due to focus on high quality (over focus on quality)
  - Supplier personnel not aware about impact on part at subsystem level
  - Manpower change led to loss of information (no defined personnel transition plan for temporary workers)
  - Upper limit on test parameter not defined (minimum no of test cycles defined, not maximum)
- Created list of action items with target dates
- Ensured all WIP actuators quarantined and sent back to supplier
- Re-educated supplier on change management
- New SOP established
- Automated and controlled testing setup at supplier end
- Reported progress every day to leadership team at Philips
- Preventive action: Supplier provided with automatic tool to test actuator
- Introduced test to monitor defect pre-shipment

**Field Actions:**
- Replaced all actuators on field after identifying change date

#### 6. Supplier Rust Problem - Root Cause Analysis
**Situation:** In one of sheet metal parts, detected rust and paint peel off in already inspected and approved parts. Alarming - how did part go past quality teams at Philips? Parts found defective are rejected right at door.

**Why?**
- Either part developed rust in warehouse (not likely - controlled conditions)
- Quality team missed it (not likely either)

**Actions:**
- Planned visit to supplier, communicated problem to management team - no breakthrough
- Spoke to supplier floor workers - no breakthrough (puzzling problem)
- Traveled to tier 2 supplier who electroplated and painted the part
- Found most relevant information from shop floor workers at tier 2 supplier (hemming, bending, plating, baking workers - workers using these processes for all their life)
- They told about electroplating problem that might be root cause: Part had gap where electroplating liquid got trapped. Trapped liquid led to internal rusting of part, later rust would grow
- Opened some parts of latest batch, saw evidence of early stage rust that corroborated root cause
- Observed in-process

**Solutions Explored:**
- Blow dry or heat every part to get rid of liquid (not feasible - lead time, doesn't assure liquid is dried, cannot be detected)
- Design change: Holes to allow liquid to seep out (impact on aesthetics)
- Eliminate feature (impacted serviceability - rough edges)
- Reduction in length of fold (allowing liquid to come out)
- Changed design to 2 90-degree bends
- Electroplating before the process

**Results:**
- Going forward, did not receive any defects
- Since puzzling new problem, shared best practices with team
- Led to process checkpoint for future paint process development

**Relevant Information:**
- Expert advice on the process
- Process flow - work and time study
